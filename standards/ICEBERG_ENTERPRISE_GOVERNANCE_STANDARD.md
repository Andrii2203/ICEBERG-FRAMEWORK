# Iceberg Enterprise Governance Standard (v1.0)
A governance framework for teams and enterprises using the Iceberg Method.

---

# 0. Purpose
This standard defines:
- roles  
- responsibilities  
- decision‑making rules  
- STOP‑CHECK governance  
- versioning rules  
- compliance requirements  

Its goal is to ensure predictable, controlled, and auditable execution across teams.

---

# 1. Roles & Responsibilities

## 1.1. Iceberg Architect
Responsible for:
- architecture  
- standards  
- protocols  
- STOP‑CHECK decisions  
- conflict resolution  

## 1.2. AI Executor
Responsible for:
- deterministic execution  
- protocol compliance  
- memory discipline  
- producing artifacts  
- STOP‑CHECK escalation  

## 1.3. Human Reviewer
Responsible for:
- reviewing artifacts  
- approving STOP‑CHECK  
- validating correctness  

## 1.4. QA Lead
Responsible for:
- SEO QA  
- Accessibility QA  
- Performance QA  
- Regression QA  

---

# 2. Decision‑Making Model

## 2.1. AI‑First Execution
AI executes all steps until STOP‑CHECK is triggered.

## 2.2. Human‑First Governance
Humans approve:
- architecture  
- deviations  
- high‑risk operations  
- final QA  

## 2.3. STOP‑CHECK Escalation
Triggered when:
- data incomplete  
- architecture unclear  
- terminology inconsistent  
- behavior ambiguous  

AI must stop and wait for approval.

---

# 3. Governance Processes

## 3.1. Architecture Approval
Requires:
- Architecture Review  
- Validation Engine  
- Human Approval  

## 3.2. Standards Compliance
Every artifact must pass:
- AI QA  
- Human QA  

## 3.3. Change Control
Any change to standards must be:
- documented  
- versioned  
- approved by Architect  

---

# 4. Versioning Governance

## 4.1. Semantic Versioning
- MAJOR — architecture changes  
- MINOR — new standards  
- PATCH — clarifications  

## 4.2. Change Log
All changes recorded in `CHANGELOG.md`.

---

# 5. Compliance
A project is compliant when:
- all standards followed  
- all protocols executed  
- all STOP‑CHECK approved  
- documentation complete
